## Why move detection out of `applyHourlyDecay`

Original code only re-checked `deathStatZeroSinceHour` on integer game-hour ticks (60 real-sec). With `graceGameHours=6` that worked, but it meant fractional grace values (and any future per-tick gameplay) couldn't be tested without waiting full game-hours, and the AI could rebound between checks. Per-tick check is cheap (4 stat reads per 500ms) and decouples death detection from decay scheduling.

## Liveness pass — action-decay + thresholds + rest + food-energy + circadian (2026-04-25 10:30, Nzib "gas semua fitur")

Goal: make the character feel alive and rhythmic. Old design had time-only stat decay → character compulsively ate/drank whenever stats nudged down because `urgencyThreshold = 20` fired on any need. Five mechanics shipped together to fix this end-to-end.

### 1. Per-tile movement decay — `TILE_DECAY`

Walking is now the main active drain. Each tile traversed: energy −0.10, thirst −0.04, hunger −0.05. `game-loop.advanceAI` measures `tilesMoved = initialBudget − leftover` per tick (correctly handles paths that empty mid-tick — old `dtSec × speed` would over-charge) and calls a new `applyTileDecay`. Effect: ~150 tiles of foraging ≈ −15 energy / −6 thirst / −7.5 hunger above the hourly clock — distance starts to matter.

### 2. Per-action costs — `ACTION_COSTS`

- `shake_tree` −2 energy
- `hunt` −3 energy + −3 thirst (sweat)
- `pickup_*` −0.3 energy (bend down)
- `cook_meat` −0.5 energy (tend the fire)
- `defecate` −0.2 energy

Applied inside `executeFinal` at the moment the verb actually happens. Hunt is now expensive enough to feel like a commitment.

### 3. Hysteresis-style triggers — `THRESHOLDS`

Replaced single `AI_CONFIG.urgencyThreshold` with per-need triggers:
- hunger ≤ 25 → eat
- thirst ≤ 25 → drink
- energy ≤ 15 → sleep (and `sleepWakeEnergy: 80 → 90` for deeper recovery)
- bladder ≥ 85 → defecate

`ai.decide` now collects all triggered needs and picks the most urgent. None firing → low-urgency mode (rest/wander). Fixes the compulsive-recovery loop.

### 4. Rest behaviour — `REST_CONFIG`

New `'rest'` action type. When in low-urgency mode, 35% chance per decide to sit instead of wander. Duration 60–180s real (random). While `currentAction === 'rest'`, `applyHourlyDecay` multiplies all drain by `decayMultiplier = 0.3` — passive recovery without explicit gain.

`'rest'` is a continuous action like `sleep`/`cook_meat` (executes inside `executeFinal`, doesn't reset to idle until duration elapses). `pendingFinal` reset-to-idle skip-list updated. Frontend reuses `man_sit` (also used by `defecate`).

### 5. Food/drink restore energy — `NUTRITION` extra fields

Eating gives a small calorie bump. Sleep stays the dominant recovery (+10/h):
- berry +1 energy (snack)
- fruit +2 energy
- meat raw +4 energy (digestion taxes it)
- meat cooked +8 energy (full meal)
- drink +0.5 energy (slight hydration boost)

### 6. Circadian rhythm — `shared/circadian.ts` NEW

Phase boundaries match the day-night visual layer keyframes. Decay (and sleep recovery) gets a per-phase multiplier so the character naturally prefers night sleep + day activity without the AI being told to.

| Phase     | Hours | Energy decay | Thirst | Hunger | Bladder | Sleep recovery |
|-----------|-------|--------------|--------|--------|---------|----------------|
| morning   | 06–12 | ×0.7         | ×1.0   | ×1.1   | ×1.2    | ×0.7           |
| afternoon | 12–18 | ×1.0         | ×1.3   | ×1.0   | ×1.0    | ×0.4 (bad nap) |
| evening   | 18–22 | ×1.2         | ×1.0   | ×1.0   | ×1.0    | ×0.6           |
| night     | 22–06 | ×1.5 (awake brutal) | ×0.6 | ×0.5 | ×0.3 | ×1.8 (deep)  |

Implementation detail: `applyHourlyDecay` walks one game-hour at a time across `lastHourKey..hourKey`, picking the phase per-hour. Most ticks see one hour gap, but this keeps phase transitions accurate when multiple hours elapse at once.

### Lessons

- Don't bake phase choice into a single tick read — walk hour-by-hour across the gap so phase boundaries get correct multipliers. Bug-prone otherwise on tick-skip frames.
- `tilesMoved = initialBudget − leftover` is the correct distance; using `dtSec × speed` would over-charge when the path runs out mid-tick.
- **Flat probability is biased when decision frequency varies.** 35% sounded like "sometimes rest" but `decide()` runs after every short wander (3-5s), each one re-rolls. Probability of rest within 5 wander cycles = `1 − 0.65⁵ = 88%`; combined with rest duration (60-180s ≫ wander 3-5s), real time-on-rest hit ~94%. Lesson: tie probability to a slowly-changing state variable (here: energy) so the roll-rate doesn't dominate the outcome.

### Tweak — energy-driven rest probability (10:40, "rest mulu")

Replaced `REST_CONFIG.probability` (flat 0.35) with `REST_CONFIG.maxProbability` (0.3) and made `ai.decide` compute:

```ts
const restProb = (1 - s.energy / 100) * REST_CONFIG.maxProbability;
```

- Energy 100 → 0% (fresh, won't sit)
- Energy 70 → 9%
- Energy 50 → 15%
- Energy 30 → 21%
- Energy 15 (sleep trigger) → ~25.5%

Verified live: 240 ticks (~2 real-min), energy 71 → 57, **19 wander entries : 1 rest entry**. Rest now reads as "char is getting tired" instead of "char is being lazy". Sleep still kicks in at energy ≤ 15 — rest is the gentle pre-sleep behaviour.


### Tweak — rest is "rare banget" (10:55, "duduk dominan banget")

User flagged that rest still felt like a system, not a flavour event. Goal: keep the sit anim because it adds life, but make it so rare an observer barely notices it. Survival outranks comfort.

Three layers in `REST_CONFIG`:

| Setting | Old | New | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| `maxProbability` | 0.30 | 0.05 | Ceiling at energy=0 is now 5% per decide tick |
| `minDurationMs` / `maxDurationMs` | 60_000–180_000 | 15_000–30_000 | When rest fires, it's a short break, not a nap |
| `energyGate` (new) | — | 60 | `ai.decide` skips the rest roll entirely while `energy >= 60` |

Plus need-interrupt in `game-loop.advanceAI`: while `currentAction === 'rest'`, if any need crosses its trigger (hunger/thirst <=25, energy<=15, bladder>=85), rest ends immediately. Survival wins over comfort.

Combined math at energy=30 (still above sleep trigger, well below gate): `restProb = (1 − 0.30) × 0.05 = 0.035` per decide tick (~1 per 12 real-sec). Most decide ticks the char wanders; a few percent of the time the char rolls a 15-30s sit. At energy ≥ 60, prob is hardcoded to zero.

## ⭐ LLM-driven feed choice — Phase 4 first contact (2026-04-25 11:45, "WTF! anjir gila broooo!! ini sejarah!")

**First time the character makes a real decision through a language model.** Old behaviour: `planFeed` was a hard-coded 10-step priority ladder — when hunger ≤ 25 it picked the first reachable kind in `[meat_cooked, meat_raw_panic, cook_meat, berry, fruit, raw_normal, hunt, fruit_ground, forage_bush, shake_tree, pickup_wood]`. Deterministic, but never reasoned. New behaviour: same threshold fires, but the *what* gets handed to a LLM choice picker that reads the character's stats + inventory + reachable options + cost annotations and picks one with a one-sentence English reasoning.

### Architecture

Threshold = "rules of nature" (hunger ≤ 25 forces action). LLM = "deciding mind" (chooses the best path from available options). The two stay decoupled — strict-real survival economics still drive the trigger, the brain only intervenes on the choice.

### Cost annotation philosophy

Each `FeedOption` carries `costs: { hungerGain?, energyDelta?, thirstDelta?, sicknessDelta?, timeMin?, note? }`. These are *hints* to the LLM, not source of truth — canonical numbers stay in `NUTRITION` / `ACTION_COSTS`. The hints let the model reason about tradeoffs ("cook_meat: takes 5min, after cook = +15 hunger safe" vs "eat_meat_raw_normal: hunger +8, sickness +20 risky") without us having to teach it the entire stat system.

### Lessons

- **Numbered options confuse small models.** `1. kind=cook_meat` made qwen3:8b output `"choice": "2"`. Removing the index fixed it instantly — the kind name alone is enough disambiguation when the prompt explicitly says "choice MUST equal one of the kind=... values".
- **Cold-start timeout matters more than warm latency.** Cold = 7s, warm = 2-3s. Set timeout for cold; once warm, `keep_alive` keeps the model resident so we never see cold again unless Ollama is idle for 10 min.
- **Async decide + tick interval mismatch needs a flag.** Tick = 500ms, LLM = 2-3s → without `decideInFlight` you queue up 4-6 concurrent calls. The flag lets the character cleanly stay idle for the few ticks an LLM call takes; the next tick after resolution reads the planted plan and starts walking.
- **Choice-first JSON schema.** Putting `"choice"` before `"reasoning"` in the schema means the model commits to the choice token before generating any rationale text — the rationale can't drift the decision via autoregressive feedback.
- **English-only I/O = ~50% fewer tokens** vs Indonesian on qwen3, plus better JSON-format adherence in testing. Player-facing copy stays Indonesian; LLM internals are English.

## Architectural pivot — strip mechanic spoilers, add observation log (afternoon)

### Symptom

After first contact, char picked `shake_tree` 25+ times in a row. Fruit kept dropping to ground (23 stacked), char never bent down to pick up. Rule-ladder days, the priority order made `pickup_fruit_ground` win after a single shake; LLM days, the model kept re-picking shake.

### Root cause

The feed prompt was telling the model how the world worked: `note: "shake → drops fruit_on_ground; pickup after"`. The model read that as "shake_tree feeds you" and never thought about the next step. We were spoon-feeding mechanics into a slot the model interpreted as a recipe.

Nzib pushed back hard: "AI ini harus paham ada aksi maka ada reaksi… bukan lo hardcode lewat prompt gitu bro." Right call — Phase 4's whole point is the agent inducing cause→effect from its own history, not reading the answer key.

### Fix

Stripped mechanic copy from the prompt entirely. Added an observation log instead:

- New `Observation` type in `choice-picker.ts`: `{t, action, target?, dHunger?, dThirst?, dEnergy?, dSickness?, inv?}`. Recorded by `game-loop` on every `executeFinal` — snapshot before, snapshot after, diff stats + inventory.
- `prompt-feed.ts` rewritten: SYSTEM_BLOCK only describes stat ranges (perceptual definitions), not mechanic rules. `formatOption` no longer emits cost/note hints — only `kind`, `target`, `dist`. `formatObservation` emits one line per past action: `- D2 14:30 forage_bush(bush_17) → energy-0.3, inv +1berry`.
- Ring buffer cap 10. First few lives will be dumb because there's nothing to learn from yet — that's expected, and the reflection layer (next section) is the durable counterpart.

### Verify

After ship + DB reset, char alternated `forage_bush → eat_berry_inv → forage_bush → eat_berry_inv`. LLM reasoning quoted the observations directly: *"Bush_14 was recently foraged and provided berries"*. The shake-spam loop is gone; the model is reading its own log to figure out what works.

### Lesson

Prompt copy that explains the world *reads as the answer*. If the goal is induction, the prompt has to leave a hole the model fills from data. Cost annotations were a benign-looking shortcut that shut off the entire learning loop. Bypass it with empirical traces (observation log) — the model still gets ground truth, but it has to actually look at the ground truth to use it.

## Reflection layer — daily rule synthesis, spirit memory across deaths (evening, "udah cukup, kita gas phase 4!")

### Goal

Phase 4 pillar #2: nightly LLM cycle reads the event log + prior rules, emits new natural-language rules, persists them lineage-scoped so they survive death. Inject those rules into the feed prompt as "Lessons from past lives" so the next decision benefits from accumulated wisdom.

### Architecture

- **Trigger:** game-day boundary inside `applyDailyRegen`. When `currentDay > previousDay && previousDay >= 1`, fire-and-forget `scheduleReflection(previousDay)`. Guard with `reflectionInFlight` flag so a slow LLM call can't pile up across multiple day rollovers.
- **Manual trigger for testing:** `POST /api/admin/reflect-now` calls `loop.triggerReflectionNow()` and returns `{ok, rulesAdded, durationMs, summary}`.
- **Inputs:** `EventRepo.loadSince(sinceMs, limit=500)` for the day's events; `RuleRepo.loadActive(lineageId)` for prior rules; current `gameDay` + `iteration`.
- **LLM call:** `runReflection(input, ollama, events, rules, log)` builds the reflection prompt, calls `generate` with `numPredict: 600, temperature: 0.6, format: 'json'`, 90s timeout (longer than feed since prompt is bigger).
- **Output:** `{rules:[{text, confidence}], summary}`. Confidence floor 0.4, max 8 rules per cycle.
- **Persistence:** `RuleRepo.save(lineageId, iter, day, rules)` inserts into the existing `rule` table (added cols `text`, `active`, `learned_at_day` via migration 003), preserves schema-compat by setting legacy `condition=''`, `effect=''`, `weight_delta=0`, `affected_actions='[]'`. `pruneActive` deactivates beyond top-10 by confidence so the prompt stays bounded.
- **Cache:** `cachedRules` on `GameLoop`, refreshed after reflection completes and on respawn. Injected into `buildFeedPrompt` as the "Lessons from past lives" block — empty until first reflection, dropping in cleanly afterward.

1. `POST /api/admin/reflect-now` →
   ```
   {"ok":true,"rulesAdded":1,"durationMs":2521,"summary":"Sleep is essential for survival."}
   ```
2. Backend log:
   ```
   [reflection] D1 iter=1 +1 rules in 2521ms — Sleep is essential for survival.
   [reflection]   • (0.90) Rest is necessary for survival
   ```

3. SQLite `rule` row: `text="Rest is necessary for survival"`, `confidence=0.9`, `active=1`, `learned_at_day=1`, `iteration=1`.

   > *"Rest is necessary for survival, but energy is already high. Shaking trees provides sustenance and energy…"*

The agent is reading its own learned wisdom, citing it explicitly, and reasoning about whether it applies to the current state. End-to-end loop closed: events → reflection → rule → prompt injection → cited in next decision.

### Why this matters

Phase 4 was always two parts. First contact (morning) gave the character a brain. The reflection layer (evening) gives the lineage a memory. A character can die now, and the next one will inherit not just stats and a fresh map, but actual sentences about what worked. Over many lives the rule set should accrete and prune — that's the spirit-memory promise from `vision.md` made concrete.

### Open

- Real game-day-boundary trigger (vs manual `/reflect-now`) hasn't fired yet on a real-time clock; will fire on next game-day rollover (24 real-min from boot). Manual trigger proves the pipeline; cron-style trigger is wired in `applyDailyRegen` and shouldn't differ.
- Sleep-gating gotcha during testing: char fell asleep before feed test could fire because energy crept low during reflection wait. Setting `energy=80` didn't wake (sleepWakeEnergy=90); had to set `energy=95`. Worth knowing for future stat-mutation testing.
- Rule pruning hasn't been stress-tested at scale yet — only one rule lives in DB so far. `pruneActive` should kick in once we hit 10+; will exercise on a multi-day live run.

### Lessons

- **Spoon-fed mechanics + induced mechanics are mutually exclusive.** The morning prompt-strip pivot and the evening reflection layer are two halves of the same shift: stop telling the model how the world works; let it learn from traces (observation log) and consolidate (reflection rules). Either alone would be weaker.
- **Schema-compat insert in legacy table > new table.** The `rule` table existed from migration 001 with old `condition/effect/weight_delta` cols. Adding `text/active/learned_at_day` columns and stuffing empty defaults into the legacy ones beats forking a parallel `rule_text` table — same query path, no JOINs, migrations stay linear.
- **Reflection feedback loop is what makes the rule "yours".** Watching the LLM cite a rule it generated, in a decision about its own current state, is qualitatively different from any prompt-engineered output. The rule isn't text we wrote — it's text the system distilled from its own life and is now using to plan its next move.

## Phase 4.7 Layer 2 — Daily Goal + Hybrid Acceptance Criteria (shipped 2026-04-25 ~18:55 GMT+7)

### Goal

Layer 2 of the goal system: per-game-day plan grounded in life goal, with sub-goals that **actually advance** as the character lives. Earlier draft of `current_step_idx` advancement was stuck at 0 across all rows because the LLM almost never emitted `completes_subgoal: true` (forward-looking prompt phrasing made it hedge to null/false).

### Changes

- **Hybrid AC schema** in `shared/types.ts` — `SubGoal.check?` discriminated union:
  - `{type:'action_performed', value:<action_kind>}` — fires when the named action's `executeFinal` runs
  - `{type:'inventory_has', item:<item>}` — fires when item lands in inventory
  - `{type:'chunk_visited_new'}` — fires on first-ever chunk entry
- **Sensor hooks in `backend/src/game-loop.ts`:**
  - `evaluateDailyGoalCheck(check)` — single dispatcher, idempotent via `dailyGoalRepo.advanceStep` (rejects when `stepIdx !== current_step_idx`)
  - `trackChunkVisit` fires `chunk_visited_new` on `isNew=true`
  - `executeFinal` fires `action_performed` per action type (sleep/rest/cook_meat early-returns + the main action switch)
  - Inventory mutations fire `inventory_changed` → evaluator checks `inventory_has`
- **LLM self-tag fallback** still runs for sub-goals without `check`. Hybrid model = structured AC primary + LLM secondary.
- **Patch C — post-advance state re-check:** after `evaluateDailyGoalCheck` advances a step, a `while` loop chains through subsequent steps whose `inventory_has` is **already satisfied by current inventory**. Reason: an item already held at goal creation would otherwise leave the next step stalled until an unrelated mutation. Only `inventory_has` is state-checkable; the others need fresh events.
- **Prompt fix in `backend/src/llm/prompt-daily-goal.ts` line 21:** reworded to forbid the LLM from putting stats (hunger/thirst/energy/sickness) and inventory item types (berry/fruit/wood/meat_raw/meat_cooked) into `referenced_entities`. Without this, every regen rejected on validation as "hallucinated entity".
- **Timeout bump in `backend/src/llm/daily-goal.ts`:** `DAILY_GOAL_TIMEOUT_MS` 90s → 120s. Hybrid-AC-augmented prompt occasionally pushed past 90s.

### Verify

End-to-end run with full DB wipe (`pre-fullwipe-20260425-190114` backup):

1. Spawned char id=1 fresh. Life goal generated: *"Explore unexplored areas to find resources"* priority 9/10.
2. Daily goal D1 generated (`alignment=advances`, 3 sub-goals): move-to-unexplored / gather-wood / rest-to-100. All 3 had valid `check` field.
3. ~9s after spawn, char crossed into a new chunk → `chunk_visited_new` fired → step 0 marked `completed:true`. `current_step_idx → 1`.
4. Patch C immediately fired (wood was already in inventory from earlier action) → step 1 marked `completed:true` via `inventory_has_state`. `current_step_idx → 2`.
5. Step 2 (`action_performed: rest`) fired naturally when energy dropped → `current_step_idx → 3`. Goal `status: completed`.
6. DB inspection confirmed all three sub-goals `completed:true`, `current_step_idx=3`.

### Why post-advance re-check (Patch C) is necessary

Naive sensor-only model: only the *currently-pointed* step gets evaluated. If the character holds wood at the moment a daily goal is generated and the goal's step 1 is "gather wood", that step **never fires** — pickup_wood event happened before the goal existed. Patch C closes this race by re-checking inventory state immediately after any advance, walking forward as far as state predicates already pass.

Only `inventory_has` is safe to chain — `chunk_visited_new` requires a fresh chunk-boundary event (state alone is insufficient since "current chunk visited" is the default state for an existing character), and `action_performed` requires an actual action firing. Conflating those would let the loop falsely complete steps.

### Lessons

- **Discriminated unions beat free-form LLM tags for state machines.** The earlier "ask the LLM to self-tag completion" approach was the intuitive ML move but produced ~0% advancement in practice. Anchoring step completion to backend-observable signals (action fired, item in inventory, chunk crossed) decoupled progress from LLM judgment quality. The LLM still chooses *what* the steps are; the backend decides *whether they happened*.
- **Always re-check world state after a state-mutating advance.** Without Patch C the system worked but felt unresponsive (steps would idle until an unrelated mutation), and the bug was invisible until a fresh-DB run.
- **Forbid stats/items as `referenced_entities` explicitly.** LLM treats stats as nouns by default. Even with a clear allowed-set, prompt phrasing is doing more than half the work.

## Debug — Game-Time Speed Multiplier (shipped 2026-04-25 ~19:50 GMT+7)

### Goal

Multi-day daily-goal observation needed real-time fast-forward. 1 game-day = 24 minutes real-time. Manual edits to `realMsPerGameMinute` per debug session was friction. Nzib: *"mending tambahin aja difrontend dan backend fitur ini, tapi ya dengan multiplier yang aman."*

### Changes

- **`backend/src/game-loop.ts`:**
  - `timeMultiplier` field, default 1, no DB persistence (resets on restart by design).
  - Helper `effectiveMsPerGameMinute() = realMsPerGameMinute / timeMultiplier`.
  - `setTimeMultiplier(m)` rebases `startMs` so `gameTime()` is **continuous** across changes — no time jumps when toggling mid-game (math: `newStartMs = now − currentGameMinutes × newEffectiveMs`).
  - All `realMsPerGameMinute` consumers in this file now read through the helper (death-watch, lifespan calc, `gameTime`). Decay rates already use game-time hour boundaries so they auto-scale.
- **`backend/src/server.ts`:**
  - `GET /api/admin/speed` → `{multiplier, allowed:[1,2,3]}`
  - `POST /api/admin/speed {multiplier:1|2|3}` → validates, rejects others with 400
- **`frontend/index.html`:** `#speed-toggle` floating panel top-right with 3 buttons (1× / 2× / 3×). Mobile: `.speed-label` hidden < 600px.
- **`frontend/src/main.ts`:** `bindSpeedToggle()` — GET on mount to sync, POST on click with optimistic active-class swap.

### Verify

- `GET /api/admin/speed` after fresh boot → `{multiplier:1, allowed:[1,2,3]}`
- `POST {multiplier:2}` → `{ok:true, multiplier:2}` → next GET reflects.
- `POST {multiplier:5}` → 400 `{ok:false, error:"multiplier must be 1, 2, or 3"}`.
- Set 3× → `docker compose restart backend` → GET reflects 1× ✅ (no persistence).
- Puppeteer e2e: click 2× button → UI active=2, backend multiplier=2 → click 1× → both revert.

### Why cap at 3×

LLM call wall-clock is anchored to real-time, not game-time. At 3× speed, game-day = 8 min real and daily-goal LLM gen (30–60s) = 6–12% of game-day — still tolerable, content quality still tracks game-state. Beyond 3× the ratio degrades fast enough that we'd be observing LLM-under-starvation behavior, not Udu behavior. Three discrete values (1×/2×/3×) force the rationale into the design itself rather than inviting >5× experimentation despite warnings.

### Lessons

- **Live multiplier needs continuity math, not just a divisor swap.** First instinct was `realMsPerGameMinute / multiplier` everywhere — but that produces a discrete game-time jump on each toggle (because `elapsedRealMs × oldMultiplier` is in a different unit than `elapsedRealMs × newMultiplier`). Rebasing `startMs` so the *current game-minute* is preserved across the change is the trick that makes mid-game speed-up feel seamless.
- **Cap range > free slider for debug knobs.** A `1–10×` slider would have invited >5× experimentation despite the warning. Three discrete values force the rationale into the design itself.
